Origins

Emerging in the early 20th century in New Orleans, jazz fused African rhythms with European harmonies. Rooted in blues and ragtime, it quickly became an influential form of expression across cultures and generations.

Signature Elements

Known for its rich harmonies, syncopated rhythms, and improvisational solos, jazz incorporates a wide variety of instruments such as saxophones, trumpets, double bass, and piano. Every performance is unique, filled with creative energy.

Influence

Jazz has laid the foundation for many modern genres, from rock to hip-hop to electronic music. Its emphasis on freedom, collaboration, and artistic expression continues to inspire musicians worldwide.
