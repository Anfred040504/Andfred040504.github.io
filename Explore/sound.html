Sound Science

Explore the physics of sound, frequencies, and how we perceive sound in the world around us.

Physics of Sound

Sound is a vibration that travels through a medium like air, water, or solid materials, creating waves of energy that we perceive as sound.

Frequencies

Frequencies determine the pitch of sound. Higher frequencies produce higher pitches, while lower frequencies result in lower pitches.

Sound Waves

Sound waves are longitudinal waves that travel through air or other media, oscillating particles back and forth to propagate the sound.

Perception of Sound

Our brain interprets sound waves, allowing us to perceive different qualities such as pitch, volume, timbre, and spatial location.

Decibels & Loudness

Decibels (dB) are used to measure the intensity or loudness of sound, with higher dB levels indicating louder sounds.

Sound in Different Environments

The way sound behaves can vary dramatically depending on the environment, such as in an open field or inside a concert hall.
