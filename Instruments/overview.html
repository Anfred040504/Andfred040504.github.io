What is the Tabla?

The Tabla is a pair of hand-played drums, each with a distinct pitch. It is used in classical Indian music and is known for its complex rhythms and expressive sound.

History & Influence

Originating in India, the Tabla has been an integral part of the Indian subcontinent's musical traditions for centuries. It plays a key role in Hindustani classical music, often accompanying vocal or instrumental performances.

Modern Use

Despite its roots in traditional music, the Tabla is used across various genres today, including fusion, world music, and even film soundtracks, maintaining its rich legacy while adapting to contemporary styles.
